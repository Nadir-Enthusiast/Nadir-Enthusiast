Larry Ellison

Founder of Oracle

American businessman and entrepreneur who was cofounder and chief executive officer (1977-2014) of the software company Oracle Corporation.

Here are some facts about Larry Ellison:

  • Lawrence Joseph Ellison was born in the Bronx on August 17, 1944
  • Ellison attended South Shore High School in Chicago
  • Ellison went to high school in Chicago's South Side before attending the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.
  • He withdrew after his sophomore year, because his adoptive mother had just died
  • In 1977 he forms the company with the investment of $2,000.
  • In 1983, the company officially became Oracle Systems Corporation after its flagship product.
  • With the defeat of Informix and of Sybase, Oracle enjoyed years of industry dominance until the rise of Microsoft SQL Server
  • In 2006, Forbes ranked him as the richest Californian.
  • The European Union approved Oracle's acquisition of Sun Microsystems on January 21, 2010
  • As of June 2020, Ellison is said to be the seventh wealthiest person in the world, with a net worth of $66.8 billion.

"Great achievers are driven, not so much by the pursuit of success, but by the fear of failure."

--Larry Ellison, American business magnate

Oracle grew from a start-up with three programmers into the largest supplier of database software and the second-largest supplier of business applications in the world.
